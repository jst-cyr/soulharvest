Animal Noises III set to roar in the nation's capital

Ottawa, ON. - September 13, 2004 - Animal Noises III, the third in a series of annual concerts to benefit the Ottawa Humane Society, is this year scheduled to feature three of Ottawa's hottest independent musical acts. Taking place on Saturday, October 9, 2004 at Babylon Nightclub (317 Bank Street), Animal Noises III will include performances by Soul Harvest, Flow14, and Splitfinger. Fulfilling the duties of host and master of ceremonies will be Eric Longley of CJOH-TV.

Based in the national capital region, the Animal Noises initiative is a local fundraising effort supported by Ottawa musicians, businesses, media outlets, and volunteers. "Ottawa's been a great city for supporting the cause of animal welfare as shown by Animal Noises raising over $2,000 last year," says Patrick Calnan, founder of the benefit concert series. This unique alliance of disparate groups across the city has produced two successful concerts and a live CD that have generated valuable financial support for the cause of animal welfare.

About the Ottawa Humane Society

The Ottawa Humane Society is a registered charity founded in 1888. The Society works in and with the community to provide leadership in the humane treatment of all animals, to address the causes of animal suffering, to encourage people to take responsibility for their animal companions, and to provide care for animals who are neglected, abused, exploited, stray, or homeless.

Animal Noises III is sponsored by: Heritage Brewing, 106.9-FM The Bear, CJOH-TV, The Ottawa SUN, Camp Fortune, Long & McQuade Musical Instruments, Miller McConnell Signs, Nutshell Music, and Lonesome Studios Productions.
