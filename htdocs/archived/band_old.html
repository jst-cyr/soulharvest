GUITAR/VOCALS: Jason St-Cyr
jstcyr@ottawa.com

Born into the servitude of a cruel world, Jason's "terrible" childhood life as part of a suburban upper-middle class family which provided him with food, shelter, good moral upbringing, and genuine manners led him to be the sorrow-stricken man known by his band-mates today. Plagued by genius and amazing looks, the Carleton University Computer Science student spends his time at pool halls and blues bars enjoying the finer points of life and a Scotch or two.

VOCALS: Kristen Lewis
klewis@ottawa.com

The beautiful and talented Kristen works hard for her money, so you better treat her right. With a voice like an angel, her singing brings life to any room, just as much as her smile. A part-time student, and full-time worker, Kristen is the voice of Soul Harvest and spends her spare time at the gym or at the bar, working for and against her beer belly.

GUITAR: Ryan Lindsay
rlindsay@ottawa.com

The young guitarist has made his mark with his flamboyant appearance and general upbeat attitude, much to the delight of fans and band-mates alike. His musicianship, heavily rooted in his rock influences, belies his age and shows the promise of a future guitar great. The former highschool football player and improv veteran is currently attending Fanshaw College in London, Ontario.

BASS: Eric St-Cyr
eric_ignition@hotmail.com

Front-man for the band Ignition, the younger of the brother duo is by far the more talented musician. Having mastered several instruments and with a few musical experiences behind him (What's Up With Chad, Beautiful Pain, etc.) this full-time working punk rocker joins Soul Harvest as a bassist and keyboardist to fill out the blues-rock sound of the band. With his trademark "dyed to the bone" hair-styles and dark bushy eyebrows, the man brings to mind images of George Pépard and that Dawson Creek fellow.

HARMONICA/VOCALS: Johnny Arthur
jonnyarthur@ottawa.com

When Earth's second moon was destroyed, the legacy of Johnny Arthur began. Known and loved by all, the secrets of his past have never been revealed. Some stories tell of great deeds, evil foes vanquished, and ladies won over. There are those who swear he beat the Devil in a show-down with a harp and a wah-wah pedal. The truth? It may never be known. This soul man brings his power and presence to the band and does his best to let the mere mortals have their share of the spotlight.

DRUMS: Chris Amos
camos@ottawa.com

The former infantry member, and current Algonquin Computer Science student, Soul Harvest's drummer Chris had never been behind a set before joining the band. Now an indispensable part of the band's music, Chris won't let you buy him a beer but throw him on a dance floor and watch those limbs fly. The frenetic Amos blames society for the misconceptions about his early years, but welcomes the chance for a new look at life.
